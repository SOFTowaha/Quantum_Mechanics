How to play

Quantum Explorer is a playful, interactive demo where you collect subatomic particles for points. This page gives controls, scoring, tips, and short facts about each particle.

Controls

  • Click or tap particles to collect them.
  • Use the arrow keys to nudge all particles in a direction. Hold Shift for a stronger nudge.
  • Press r to reset particle positions.
  • Use the Shuffle button to randomize positions for surprise spawns.

Scoring and Levels

Each particle has a point value. Collecting particles increases your score. When your score exceeds a multiple of 200, you level up — leveling gives a small animation and increases the game's pace.

Particle guide

Electron (common)
Small, negative lepton. Worth 10 points.
Up Quark (uncommon)
Constituent of protons. Worth 25 points.
Down Quark (uncommon)
Pairs with up quarks in baryons. Worth 25 points.
Photon (rare)
Quantum of light. Worth 75 points — keep an eye out.
Gluon (rare)
Carrier of the strong force. Worth 90 points and very rare here.
Muon / Tau / Neutrinos (special)
Occasionally spawned as special events — worth bonus points and may trigger SFX.

Tips

  • Watch for rarity badges near particles — rare ones are high-value targets.
  • Use arrow nudges to herd multiple particles together for fast multi-collects.
  • Shuffle when the field becomes crowded to respawn interesting particles.
